History

SSS was established in 2018 by a coalition of community members including homeless advocates, civic leaders, members of the clergy, committed activists, and neighbors. This non-profit organization developed after two years of volunteer direct service to the unhoused members of Silverton and the surrounding areas.

The Silverton Area Warming Shelter was established in December 2016 in response to an incoming snow storm. The shelter operated throughout the winter of 2016/2017, and continued the following year, housed at Oak Street Church and Immanuel Lutheran Church. Dozens of volunteers staffed the shelter overnight, prepared meals, laundered bedding, and led outreach efforts into area camps. Shelter organizers provided advocacy services to the unhoused throughout the year on a volunteer basis, cultivating relationships within the homeless community, advocating for increased services and community response.

The shelter developed an Advisory Board in 2017, which concluded that a non-profit corporation dedicated to serving people experiencing homelessness in Silverton was needed to meet the needs of the community. Silverton Sheltering Services was born out of this work.
